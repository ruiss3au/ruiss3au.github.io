Gothenburg in February was the mirror image of Gothenburg in January. RAN1#124
had been the 6G design peak, with FS_6G_Radio consuming half the meeting.
RAN4#118, held two months later in the same city, showed RAN4 beginning to
catch up. Rel-20 grew from 15 percent at Dallas to 27 percent. FS_6G_Radio grew
from 230 to 371 contributions. Rel-20 carrier aggregation work began. The Rel-19
-Perf work continued but was no longer the dominant force.

The 27 percent Rel-20 share was still far from RAN1's 81 percent a month
earlier. The structural lag persisted. But the growth rate was accelerating:
Dallas had been the inflection point, and Gothenburg confirmed the trend.

## FS_6G_Radio Accelerates in RAN4

FS_6G_Radio's growth from Dallas reflected both more contributors engaging with
6G and deeper technical content. Where Dallas had been about specification
efficiency and framework questions, Gothenburg added specific technical studies:
channel bandwidth implications for RF requirements, MPR and ACLR trade-offs
for 6G waveforms, and RRM measurement framework design for the 7-24 GHz range.

vivo contributed on frequency range definitions and spectrum regulatory survey
for 6G [R4-2600669](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600669.zip), the foundational spectrum scoping work. Ericsson
contributed on channel and synchronization raster arrangements [R4-2601382](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601382.zip)
and provided input to the ECC PT1 request for AAS BS operating below 1 GHz
[R4-2601079](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601079.zip), linking RAN4's 6G work to European regulatory processes. Nokia
contributed on frequency ranges and spectrum regulatory survey [R4-2601665](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601665.zip)
and on band and band combination definition and simplification [R4-2601666](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601666.zip).
Nokia also filed initial simulation results on transmitter EVM relaxation and
its impacts on the BS receiver [R4-2601765](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601765.zip), a direct RAN4 contribution to

The meeting also saw the first RAN4-driven AI/ML use case proposals. CATT
contributed on AI-based SRS power imbalance compensation [R4-2600251](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600251.zip) and

improve RF performance in specific transmitter and receiver scenarios without
requiring changes to the air interface specification. These were RAN4-native
AI proposals, independent of the RAN1 AI/ML work, reflecting RAN4's growing
confidence in defining its own AI/ML scope.

## Rel-20 Carrier Aggregation Begins

The most significant new development at RAN4#118 was the appearance of Rel-20
carrier aggregation work items. NR_CADC_SUL_R20_HPUE and DC_xBLTE_yBNR_R20_HPUE
were the Rel-20 equivalents of the Rel-19 carrier aggregation work that had been
a constant background thread through every RAN4 meeting.

### HPUE for Rel-20 CA

NR_CADC_SUL_R20_HPUE addressed high-power UE operation for Rel-20 carrier
aggregation and supplementary uplink. HPUE (power class 2, 26 dBm) is critical
for the 7-24 GHz range because the higher path loss at these frequencies
requires higher UE transmit power to maintain coverage
[R4-2601776](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601776.zip).

Samsung discussed adaptive changes with HPUE notes removal in Rel-19 and HPUE
request handling in Rel-20 [R4-2600371](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600371.zip). Apple contributed CSI-RS based
L1-RSRP accuracy requirements for neighbor cells in FR1 [R4-2600558](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600558.zip) and FR2
[R4-2600559](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600559.zip), the RRM measurement refinement work that underpins RRM Phase 6.

Ericsson filed draft Big CRs for TS 38.101-1 and TS 38.101-3 for Rel-20 HPUE
carrier aggregation [R4-2601776](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601776.zip), [R4-2601777](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601777.zip), defining MPR and A-MPR values
for the first set of Rel-20 band combinations. Nokia filed draft CRs on power
class 2 and power class 1.5 in CA combinations [R4-2601672](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601672.zip), [R4-2602217](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2602217.zip).

When a UE transmits simultaneously on a 3.5 GHz carrier and a 15 GHz carrier,
third-order intermodulation products fall at widely separated frequencies
(approximately 8 GHz and 26.5 GHz), far from the receiver bands at either
3.5 GHz or 15 GHz. This is easier than sub-7 GHz CA where intermodulation
products can fall directly in the receiver band. The Rel-20 HPUE MPR values
were correspondingly less restrictive than Rel-19 values, allowing higher
transmit power on 7-24 GHz carriers
[R4-2601777](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601777.zip).

### DC R20: Dual Connectivity for the 6G Era

DC_xBLTE_yBNR_R20_HPUE continued the EN-DC and NR-DC dual connectivity work
into Rel-20. The work item defined new band combinations that include 7-24 GHz
NR carriers aggregated with LTE or NR anchor carriers. The dual connectivity
framework was particularly important for the 6G transition: early 6G deployments
would use EN-DC or NR-DC with a sub-7 GHz anchor for coverage and a 7-24 GHz
secondary cell for capacity.

## Single Cell Multi-Carrier Reaches RAN4

The SCMC ("Gothia-cell") concept that had emerged at RAN1#122 in Bangalore arrived in RAN4
at Gothenburg. MediaTek contributed views on single carrier output power
requirements for 6G [R4-2601051](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601051.zip), and Sony filed parallel views [R4-2601401](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601401.zip).
Spreadtrum proposed that RAN4 study SCMC UE architectures: single RF chain
versus partial shared RF chain, intra-band versus inter-band feasibility, and
applicability of measurements from the anchor carrier (CC1) to the data
carriers (CC2, CC3).

SCMC represents a philosophical shift: from treating spectrum as independent
cells to treating it as a single pool controlled from one anchor. The concept
simplifies network operation but raises RF feasibility questions: can a UE
accurately measure the anchor carrier and extrapolate those measurements to a
data carrier at a fundamentally different frequency? The RAN4 discussion was
just beginning.

## AI/ML Mobility: RAN4's Normative Work Expands

NR_AIML_Mob-Core, which had debuted at Dallas, grew to become a significant work
item at Gothenburg. The normative work now covered specific RRM measurement
configurations: how the UE reports an AI/ML-predicted cell quality measurement
alongside the conventional SSB-based measurement, what confidence thresholds the
UE applies before using the predicted measurement for cell reselection or
handover reporting, and how the network configures the trade-off between
prediction accuracy and measurement overhead.

The AI/ML mobility work benefited from RAN1 consensus that mobility prediction
is a vendor-differentiation feature, not a standardized model, allowing RAN4
to focus on measurement reporting frameworks without mandating model
architectures [R4-2600251](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600251.zip), [R4-2600250](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600250.zip).

## Rel-19 -Perf Work Winds Down

The remaining Rel-19 work was issue resolution: closing the last open points,
correcting errors in previously agreed CRs, and finalizing the conformance test
procedures. NR_demod_Ph6-Perf contributed to the SCM-based testing framework.
Nokia filed simulation results for 6Rx UE performance with interference
[R4-2600403](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600403.zip). Samsung discussed FR1 SU-MIMO performance requirements with
spatial channel model [R4-2600714](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2600714.zip), and Sanechips contributed parallel
discussion on demodulation requirements [R4-2601243](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_118/Docs/R4-2601243.zip).

## Company Dynamics: Ericsson Leads at Home

Ericsson led the contribution count at its home meeting for the second month in
a row. HiSilicon held second. Sanechips rose to third, driven by UE chipset
contributions to the Rel-20 carrier aggregation work. Nokia dropped to fourth,
reflecting lower engagement with Rel-20 carrier aggregation. OPPO rose
significantly, joining the upper tier for the first time in the RAN4 series.

## Looking Ahead to RAN1#125

RAN4#118 was the meeting where RAN4's Rel-20 program became self-sustaining.
FS_6G_Radio, Rel-20 carrier aggregation, AI/ML mobility, and IoT NTN Phase 2
formed a coherent Rel-20 work program that no longer depended on Rel-19
momentum. The next meeting is RAN1#125, the final RAN1 meeting before the
RAN#112 plenary that would freeze the Rel-20 Stage-2 scope.
