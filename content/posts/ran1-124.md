# RAN1#124 - January 2026, Gothenburg, Sweden

Gothenburg was the meeting where 6G swallowed RAN1. FS_6G_Radio accounted for
more than half of all contributions. Rel-20 contributions outnumbered Rel-19 by
five to one. The Rel-19 work items that had been RAN1's primary activity six
months earlier in Bangalore had either closed or reduced to maintenance levels.
RAN1 was now a 6G design factory, and the Gothenburg meeting was its peak
production run.

The meeting was held in Ericsson's home city, and the company took the
opportunity to lead the contribution count. But the distribution remained
characteristically flat for RAN1, with six companies within a narrow margin at
the top. The 6G work had entered a phase where companies were submitting
detailed technical specifications: waveform parameter sets, MIMO codebook
designs, channel model calibration results. The brainstorming phase was over.
The engineering phase had begun.

# FS_6G_Radio: Half the Meeting

FS_6G_Radio at 931 contributions was not just the largest work item at this
meeting. It was larger than the entire RAN1#123 meeting in Arlington. The
volume reflected both the breadth of the 6G study (waveforms, numerologies,
MIMO, channel models, AI/ML, sensing, energy efficiency, coexistence) and its
depth (each topic had progressed from candidate proposals to detailed
simulation results and parameter trade-off analysis).

## Waveform Down-Select Approaches Decision

The 6G waveform debate reached its decisive phase at Gothenburg. After three
meetings of proposals, simulations, and moderator summaries, the candidate set
had narrowed to a primary waveform and an optional enhancement. OFDM with
enhanced spectral shaping was selected as the baseline 6G waveform, maintaining
backward compatibility with NR signal processing chains while adding spectral
containment improvements that reduce guard band overhead. OTFS was designated as
an optional waveform for high-Doppler scenarios, available when configured by
the network but not required for baseline UE implementation.

The decision represented a victory for the evolutionary approach advocated by
Ericsson, Qualcomm, and Apple over the clean-slate approach advocated by Nokia
and some Asian contributors. The argument that won was practical: a new waveform
requires new silicon, new test equipment, new deployment tools, and new operator
training. An enhanced OFDM leverages the existing NR ecosystem while delivering
most of the spectral efficiency gain that a new waveform would provide.

## Waveform Comparison Studies

The waveform candidates were evaluated through comprehensive simulation
campaigns that compared OTFS, UFMC, filtered-OFDM, and enhanced-OFDM against
the agreed KPI framework, building on the waveform discussion papers filed at
this meeting [R1-2600054](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600054.zip), [R1-2600432](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600432.zip), [R1-2600925](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600925.zip). OTFS showed a clear
advantage at Doppler shifts above 2 kHz, achieving 20-30 percent higher
throughput than OFDM in high-speed train scenarios at 500 km/h. But at
pedestrian speeds (3 km/h), OTFS had a 5-8 percent spectral efficiency
penalty relative to OFDM [R1-2600838](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600838.zip). UFMC reduced out-of-band emissions by
15 dB but required twice the transmitter filter complexity. Enhanced-OFDM
with time-domain windowing achieved 10 dB out-of-band reduction at half the
UFMC filter complexity, making it the practical compromise
[R1-2600311](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600311.zip).

## Channel Models for Sub-THz

The sub-THz channel models defined at Gothenburg included: indoor hotspot
scenarios where line-of-sight propagation dominates and data rates above 100
Gbps are achievable at ranges up to 10 meters, outdoor urban micro scenarios
where diffraction and reflection from building surfaces provide coverage but
atmospheric absorption limits range to approximately 100 meters, and fixed
wireless access scenarios where high-gain directional antennas at both ends
extend range to approximately 500 meters.

Nokia contributed updated calibration simulation results that underpinned these
channel models [R1-2506382](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2506382.zip), with HiSilicon [R1-2505225](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2505225.zip) providing additional
calibration data and Samsung [R1-2505549](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2505549.zip) addressing remaining issues.

## Massive MIMO for 6G

The 6G MIMO work defined antenna configurations that exceed NR's maximum of 64
antenna elements per panel. 6G massive MIMO targets 128 to 256 elements per
panel for the 7-24 GHz range, with hybrid beamforming architectures that
combine analog beamforming (phase shifters) with digital precoding (baseband
processing) to manage the power consumption and cost of fully digital
beamforming at these array sizes. For sub-THz frequencies above 100 GHz, the
MIMO approach shifts to lens-based beamforming where a dielectric lens focuses
the signal onto an antenna array.

Nokia contributed overall aspects of the 6G multi-antenna system
[R1-2600031](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600031.zip), OPPO discussed general aspects and frameworks
[R1-2600186](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600186.zip), NVIDIA contributed frameworks for multi-antenna system
[R1-2600593](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600593.zip), InterDigital discussed beam management and TCI framework
[R1-2600852](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_124/Docs/R1-2600852.zip), and CAICT discussed 6G multi-antenna system design

# Single Cell Multi-Carrier (SCMC)

SCMC was not discussed at this meeting. The concept first appeared at RAN1#122
in Bangalore (July 2025), where OPPO's modular air interface design mentioned
single-cell multiple-carrier scenarios in the context of BWP
[R1-2505758](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_122/Docs/R1-2505758.zip). It resurfaced at RAN4#118 in Gothenburg
(February 2026) under spectrum aggregation and was formally named the
"Gothia-cell" at RAN4#119 in Dalian (May 2026)
[R4-2605978](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_119/Docs/R4-2605978.zip). The nickname refers to
RAN4#118's Gothenburg location, not this RAN1 meeting.

# AI/ML Phase 2: Case 0 Specification Nears Completion

The AI/ML air interface Phase 2 work reached near-completion for Case 0 (CSI
spatial/frequency compression without temporal prediction). The inference
parameters, tensor formats, and performance requirements had been agreed. The
remaining open issue was the training data framework. The working assumption at
Gothenburg was that the specification would define the inference interface and
leave the training framework to an industry collaboration outside 3GPP.

# MIMO Phase 6: Distributed MIMO Codebooks

MIMO Phase 6 progressed to specific codebook designs for distributed MIMO. The
solution adopted at Gothenburg used a two-stage codebook: the first stage
selects the strongest TRPs (typically two or three out of the available set),
and the second stage quantizes the channel from the selected TRPs using an
enhanced version of the NR Type II codebook.

# NR Coverage Phase 3: Filling the Gaps

NR_cov_Ph3-Core was a new Rel-20 work item that appeared for the first time at
Gothenburg. Coverage Phase 3 addressed coverage limitations identified in NR
deployments: uplink coverage at cell edge for the 7-24 GHz range where higher
path loss reduces range, indoor coverage from outdoor cells, and coverage for
new device classes (RedCap, ambient IoT, NTN handheld) that have lower transmit
power or simpler receivers.

HiSilicon contributed coverage enhancements for NR Phase 3

# Company Dynamics: Ericsson Leads at Home

Ericsson led the contribution count at its home meeting, followed closely by
OPPO, Samsung, CATT, Nokia, HiSilicon, and Apple in a tight pack. OPPO's rise
to second place was notable. InterDigital, Ofinno, Spreadtrum, and NEC
maintained their mid-tier presence, contributing to specific technical areas
where they had expertise.

# Looking Ahead to RAN4#118

RAN1#124 was the peak of 6G physical layer design activity. The waveform
decision, the sub-THz channel models, the massive MIMO configurations, the
distributed MIMO codebooks, the sensing signal designs, and the AI/ML Case 0
completion represented the core technical decisions that would define the 6G
air interface. RAN4#118, back in Gothenburg two months later, would receive
this output as a cascade of liaison statements and work item descriptions.
