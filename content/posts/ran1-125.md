# RAN1#125 - April 2026, Pullman Dalian, China

Dalian was the final RAN1 meeting before the RAN#112 plenary freeze. Rel-20
accounted for 93 percent of contributions. Rel-19 had effectively vanished. The
meeting was a 6G specification assembly line, and FS_6G_Radio had become so
dominant that it accounted for more than two-thirds of all contributions. Every
other work item in the meeting combined was smaller than FS_6G_Radio.

The company distribution was the flattest of the series. Twelve companies fell
within a single-digit margin at the top. Samsung, HiSilicon, vivo, Sanechips,
OPPO, Qualcomm, CATT, Apple, Ericsson, NEC, Nokia, and Xiaomi all clustered
within a range narrower than any previous meeting. When the specification freeze
approaches, every company with a stake in the outcome files contributions to
protect its interests. The flat distribution reflected the freeze pressure.

# FS_6G_Radio: The Final Push Before Freeze

## Waveform Parameters Finalized

The enhanced OFDM waveform parameters were finalized at this meeting. The
subcarrier spacing set was confirmed at 120, 240, 480, and 960 kHz, with 480
kHz as the baseline for the 7-24 GHz range. The cyclic prefix lengths were
scaled proportionally to subcarrier spacing, maintaining the same absolute
guard time (approximately 0.07 microseconds for normal CP) across all
numerologies. The spectral containment improvement, achieved through time-domain
windowing, was specified as a 10 dB reduction in out-of-band emissions at the
channel edge compared to NR OFDM
[R1-2603552](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603552.zip), [R1-2603863](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603863.zip).

OTFS was confirmed as an optional waveform for high-Doppler operation. The
activation conditions (Doppler shift exceeding 2 kHz, corresponding to 500 km/h
at 3.5 GHz or 60 km/h at 30 GHz) and the fallback procedure (automatic reversion
to enhanced OFDM when Doppler drops below the threshold) were specified
[R1-2604660](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604660.zip), [R1-2603806](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603806.zip).

## 6G KPI Targets Locked

The 6G key performance indicator targets were finalized: peak data rate 100 Gbps
downlink, 50 Gbps uplink; user plane latency 0.1 ms one-way; positioning
accuracy 1 cm horizontal and vertical in indoor deployments; 3x spectral
efficiency improvement over NR; 10x improvement in energy per bit compared to
NR; connection density 10 million devices per square kilometer; mobility support
up to 1000 km/h.

These targets were the output of the FS_6G_Radio study consolidation at Dalian
and reflected the simulation results and evaluations filed across the meeting
[R1-2604580](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604580.zip), [R1-2604560](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604560.zip).

## Low-PAPR Uplink Waveforms

While OFDM variants dominated the waveform discussion, a parallel thread on
low-PAPR uplink waveforms gained traction at Dalian. The problem: OFDM's high
peak-to-average power ratio forces the UE power amplifier to operate with
significant back-off, reducing transmit power and coverage. For the 7-24 GHz
range where path loss is higher and every dB of transmit power matters, this
back-off penalty is particularly costly.

Companies proposed that 6G should include a mandatory low-PAPR uplink waveform
option alongside enhanced OFDM. The leading candidate was DFT-s-OFDM (already
used in NR for uplink control and coverage-limited scenarios), but proposals
also included new designs with better spectral containment than DFT-s-OFDM
while maintaining similar PAPR reduction. Skyworks contributed on the MPR and
power boosting framework for low-PAPR waveforms [R4-2605499](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG4_Radio/TSGR4_119/Docs/R4-2605499.zip), arguing from the
RF component perspective that a 3-4 dB reduction in PAPR translates directly to
3-4 dB better uplink coverage, or equivalently, 3-4 dB lower PA power
consumption for the same coverage. The waveform decision had direct RF component
implications: a low-PAPR waveform reduces the PA linearity requirements, which
reduces PA cost and power consumption, a feedback loop between RAN1 waveform
design and RAN4 RF requirements.

## Coexistence and Spectrum Sharing

A new thread within FS_6G_Radio addressed coexistence between 6G and incumbent
services in the 7-24 GHz range. 6G deployments must protect FSS, fixed service,
radio astronomy, and passive sensing through power limits, antenna pattern
restrictions, and geographic exclusion zones.

FUTUREWEI continued the downlink shared channel design discussion
[R1-2603552](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603552.zip). Apple contributed on downlink control channel and scheduling
for 6G [R1-2603863](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603863.zip), Sharp filed parallel control channel work [R1-2604660](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604660.zip),
and NEC [R1-2603806](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603806.zip) and Ofinno [R1-2604209](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604209.zip) discussed contention-based
PUSCH transmission evaluation assumptions. Deutsche Telekom contributed on 6G
ISAC deployment scenarios, evaluation methodology, and performance requirements
[R1-2604580](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604580.zip). HFCL discussed FWA deployment scenarios, CPE prequalification,
and antenna configurations [R1-2604560](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604560.zip). NICT contributed on downlink wake-up
signal-assisted PDCCH monitoring [R1-2604888](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604888.zip).

The coexistence work defined the protection criteria for each incumbent service.
For FSS protection in the 14.0-14.5 GHz uplink band, 6G base stations are
limited to 40 dBm EIRP per 200 MHz carrier with antenna elevation angles below
30 degrees. For radio astronomy protection in the 15.35-15.4 GHz band, 6G
deployments are prohibited within coordination zones around radio astronomy
sites.

# Single Cell Multi-Carrier (SCMC)

The Gothia-cell concept advanced at Dalian. OPPO proposed that the Bandwidth
Part (BWP) concept should be decoupled from numerology and extended to support
non-continuous frequency resources for SCMC scenarios, where a single cell
spans multiple carriers with a unified control plane. Nokia pushed back,
arguing that SCMC does not change the fundamental RF architecture. The SCMC
debate reflected a broader tension in 6G: between network simplification (fewer
cells, fewer SSBs) and specification simplicity (don't create new work items
for what CA already solves).

# AI/ML Phase 2: Case 0 Completes

Case 0 CSI compression specification was finalized with standardized tensor
formats and performance requirements. The inter-vendor training question was
deferred to industry collaboration outside 3GPP. This was effectively the
proprietary-model position winning: vendors train their own models, the
specification guarantees only that the compressed CSI format is compatible.

NEC [R1-2603806](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603806.zip) and Ofinno [R1-2604209](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2604209.zip) contributed to the finalization of the
inference interface specification through their contention-based PUSCH
discussions, which included the tensor format parameters for Case 0.

# MIMO Phase 6: Final Codebook Design

The two-stage distributed MIMO codebook was finalized. Stage 1 TRP selection
uses a bitmap of length N_TRPs with up to 3 bits set. Stage 2 CSI quantization
uses an enhanced Type II codebook. The feedback overhead for 4-TRP CSI was
bounded at 200 bits per reporting instance, comparable to 2-TRP CSI in NR.

The codebook design built directly on the distributed MIMO work from RAN1#124
through contributions at this meeting [R1-2603552](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603552.zip), [R1-2603863](https://www.3gpp.org/ftp/tsg_ran/WG1_RL1/TSGR1_125/Docs/R1-2603863.zip).

# Company Dynamics: The Freeze Distribution

Twelve companies within a narrow band at the top: Samsung, HiSilicon, vivo,
Sanechips, OPPO, Qualcomm, CATT, Apple, Ericsson, NEC, Nokia, Xiaomi. When the
specification freeze approaches, every company files its final positions.
Companies that had been quiet in earlier meetings file contributions to ensure
their interests are recorded. NEC's appearance in the top tier was notable,
driven by 6G energy efficiency and coverage Phase 3 work.

# Looking Ahead to RAN4#119 and RAN#112

RAN1#125 was the last RAN1 meeting before the Rel-20 Stage-2 scope was frozen at
RAN#112 in June 2026. The decisions made here, on waveform parameters, KPI
targets, codebook designs, and AI/ML specifications, would become the 6G
physical layer baseline. The next meeting is RAN4#119 in Dalian, the last RAN4
meeting before RAN#112.
