Dallas was the breakout. After four meetings where Rel-20 was measured in single
digits, where it was scoping papers and positioning statements rather than
technical work, FS_6G_Radio arrived in RAN4 with the force of a dam breaking.
The work item that had consumed RAN1's attention since Bangalore landed on
RAN4's agenda with the largest single-item volume of any RAN4 meeting in the
series. The RAN1-RAN4 lag had compressed: RAN1#122 had launched 6G studies in
July, and five months later RAN4 was organizing its own 6G specification
improvement program.

The Rel-19 work was still present but in a fundamentally different register. The
-Core work items that had dominated RAN4#113-116 had shifted to -Perf. SBFD
was no longer NR_duplex_evo-Core at 106 contributions; it was
NR_duplex_evo-Perf at 70 contributions. MIMO Phase 5 had shifted from -Core to
-Perf. NTN Phase 3, ATG enhancements, mobility Phase 4 were all in their -Perf
phase. The group was no longer writing requirements; it was writing tests.

# FS_6G_Radio Arrives in RAN4

FS_6G_Radio's arrival in RAN4 was the most significant structural event since
the series began. After four RAN4 meetings that were essentially a Rel-19
delivery machine, the 6G study item made RAN4 a participant in the 6G design
conversation rather than just an eventual recipient of RAN1's decisions.

## RAN4's Take on 6G: Specification Efficiency

RAN4's approach to 6G was fundamentally different from RAN1's. Where RAN1
discussed waveforms, numerologies, and modulation schemes, RAN4 discussed
specification efficiency: how to write better specifications, how to reduce
the testing burden, how to make conformance frameworks that scale to the wider
bandwidths, more antennas, and more frequency bands of 6G.

MediaTek contributed considerations on scalable and forward-compatible design
for diverse device types [R4-2520815], arguing that 6G specifications should be
designed to accommodate device classes that do not yet exist. The NR
specifications were written for a three-tier device model (premium, mid-range,
low-end), and each new device type (RedCap, ambient IoT, NTN handheld, VSAT)
required retrofitting the specification framework. 6G should learn from this:
the conformance framework should be parameterized from the start to support
arbitrary device capabilities rather than hard-coded for specific device classes.

Nokia contributed on RAN4 6G operation efficiency enhancement for demodulation
specifications [R4-2520530], proposing that demodulation performance
requirements should be specified as functions of channel model parameters rather
than enumerated for each channel model. In NR, a UE must pass separate
demodulation tests for EPA, EVA, ETU, TDL-A/B/C, and CDL-A/B/C channel models,
each with its own requirement. Nokia's proposal was to specify demodulation
requirements as a continuous function of delay spread and Doppler spread,
allowing a single requirement to cover all channel models and simplifying the
test matrix.

MediaTek also contributed on 6G spectrum candidates and band combination
simplification [R4-2520818], addressing the combinatorial explosion of band
combinations that had plagued NR. Each new frequency band multiplies the number
of band combinations that must be tested for carrier aggregation, EN-DC, and
SUL. 6G should define band combination rules that limit the testing burden to a
manageable subset of the possible combinations.

CATT filed a further discussion on general aspects for 6G operation efficiency
[R4-2520182] and coordinated the running summary of 6G operation efficiency
after the RAN4#116bis meeting [R4-2520187]. Sanechips contributed considerations
on 6G RAN4 UE and BS RF specification enhancement [R4-2521817] and general
issues for 6G RAN4 specification improvement [R4-2521816]. CMCC discussed
demodulation specification enhancement for 6G study [R4-2520441], and CATT
filed RRM aspects for RAN4 6G specification enhancement [R4-2520121].

The common thread across these contributions was that 6G was an opportunity to
fix the specification problems that had accumulated through five releases of NR.
The NR specification framework, designed in Rel-15 for a relatively simple
device and deployment model, had been stretched by each subsequent release. 6G
was the chance to redesign the framework for the deployment complexity that was
now understood.

## Waveform Implications for RF Requirements

Ericsson contributed on system parameters and UE transmit assumptions for 6G
waveforms [R4-2521134], the most directly RAN4-relevant 6G contribution at this
meeting. The waveform candidates being evaluated in RAN1 (OTFS, UFMC, filtered
OFDM) have different peak-to-average power ratios, different spectral regrowth
characteristics, and different sensitivity to power amplifier nonlinearity than
the OFDM baseline used in NR. These differences translate directly into
different MPR values, different ACLR requirements, and different EVM targets.

Ericsson's contribution proposed that RAN4 should define a waveform-agnostic
framework for transmitter requirements, where the specific MPR, ACLR, and EVM
values are functions of the waveform parameters (subcarrier spacing, modulation
order, resource block allocation) rather than hard-coded for a single waveform.
This would allow the specification to accommodate whatever waveform RAN1
selected without requiring RAN4 to rewrite its requirements.

# The -Perf Transition: Rel-19 Moves to Testing

The shift from -Core to -Perf across multiple work items was the structural
story of Dallas for Rel-19. The -Perf suffix indicates that the work item is
defining conformance test procedures and performance requirements, as opposed to
-Core which defines the functional and RF requirements themselves. The
transition meant that the features RAN4 had been specifying since Orlando were
now stable enough to test.

## MIMO Phase 5 Conformance

NR_MIMO_Ph5-Perf was the second-largest work item at Dallas, representing the
conformance testing phase for the MIMO Phase 5 requirements that had been
specified in earlier meetings. The -Perf work covered test procedures for
coherent joint transmission from two TRPs, 32-port CSI-RS codebook testing, and
unified TCI framework verification. These were the practical test definitions
that would allow certification labs to verify that a UE or gNB implements MIMO
Phase 5 correctly.

## SBFD Performance Testing

NR_duplex_evo-Perf was the third-largest work item, the conformance testing
counterpart to the SBFD specification work that had consumed RAN4#113-116.
The -Perf work covered test procedures for CLI measurement accuracy, SBFD
slot format verification, and BS self-interference cancellation performance.
These were the tests that would determine whether a vendor's SBFD implementation
met the specification requirements.

The shift to -Perf for SBFD was particularly significant because SBFD was the
most technically demanding Rel-19 feature. The self-interference cancellation
requirement, the CLI measurement procedures, and the slot format configurations
all needed to be verified through conformance testing before operators would
deploy the feature. The -Perf phase was the bridge between specification and
deployment.

# New Rel-20 Work Items Debut

Three new Rel-20 work items made their first appearance at Dallas, representing
the beginning of RAN4's own Rel-20 specification program beyond the 6G study
item.

## AI/ML Mobility Goes Normative

NR_AIML_Mob-Core appeared as a normative work item, the RAN4 counterpart to the
FS_NR_AIML_Mob study item that RAN1 had been working on since Athens. The -Core
suffix indicated that RAN4 was defining RF and RRM requirements, not just
studying testability. The mobility AI/ML work had graduated from study to
specification.

The normative work focused on defining RRM measurement configurations for
AI/ML-assisted mobility: how the UE reports AI/ML-predicted cell quality, how
the network configures the prediction confidence thresholds, and how the
existing RRM measurement framework accommodates AI/ML-generated measurements
alongside conventional SSB-based measurements.

CATT discussed impacts of AI/ML RRM measurement prediction [R4-2520151] and
measurement event prediction [R4-2520152] on core requirements. Xiaomi
contributed on measurement prediction [R4-2520489] and measurement event
prediction [R4-2520490] for AI mobility. CMCC discussed RRM requirements for
indirect measurement event prediction [R4-2520452], and Apple contributed on
RAN4 impacts for measurement event prediction in AI/ML mobility [R4-2520622].

## RRM Phase 6: Apple's Vision Becomes a Work Item

NR_RRM_Ph6-Core was the realization of Apple's Rel-20 scoping paper that had
been filed at every RAN4 meeting since Athens. After four meetings of pitching,
Apple's RRM Phase 6 became a formal work item. The Phase 6 scope, as defined at
Dallas, covered AI/ML-assisted mobility measurements, multi-TRP measurement
frameworks for distributed MIMO, and measurement gap enhancements for the 7-24
GHz range. Early contributions addressed (e)RedCap UE enhancement: CATT
discussed RRM requirements for (e)RedCap UE enhancement [R4-2520118], OPPO
contributed parallel requirements [R4-2521329], China Telecom discussed
operator perspectives [R4-2520805], and Nokia filed RRM requirements for
Rel-20 (e)RedCap UE enhancements [R4-2521603].

The work item's establishment was a significant victory for Apple, which had
been the primary advocate for RRM Phase 6 since the first scoping papers. The
work item would define measurement procedures that directly affect the user
experience of every 5G and 6G device.

## IoT NTN Phase 2: The Next Round

NR_IoT_NTN_req_Ph2-Core continued the IoT NTN testing work into a second phase.
The Phase 1 work (NR_IoT_NTN_req_test_enh) had focused on the 3 MHz channel
bandwidth and HPUE for NTN. Phase 2 expanded the scope to additional NTN
frequency bands, additional device classes, and enhanced testing procedures.

MediaTek discussed RRM core requirements for NTN requirement enhancement Phase 2
[R4-2520039]. Samsung contributed on RRM requirements for HD-FDD for Ku-band
with FR1 numerology [R4-2520227] and on UE RF requirements for the same
scenario [R4-2521423]. vivo provided a general discussion on RRM impact for
HD-FDD in Ku-band with FR1 numerology [R4-2521442]. Ericsson discussed RRM
requirements for enhanced requirements and test methodology for NR and IoT NTN
[R4-2521222].

# Carrier Aggregation: The Constant Thread

Carrier aggregation (NR_CADC_SUL_R19-Core) remained a top-five work item at
Dallas, as it had been at every RAN4 meeting in the series. The work was
procedural: new band combinations, new power class definitions, new MPR and
A-MPR values for each combination. Carrier aggregation is the RAN4 equivalent of
background radiation: always present, always consuming a significant fraction of
the contribution volume, never making headlines.

The Rel-20 equivalent (NR_CADC_SUL_R20_HPUE) had not yet appeared, but the
Rel-19 carrier aggregation work was laying the groundwork for the Rel-20 band
combinations that would include 7-24 GHz carriers.

# Company Dynamics: Ericsson Holds, Apple Rises Further

Ericsson maintained the top position with a clear lead over HiSilicon, who held
second. Nokia rose to nearly tie HiSilicon. The infrastructure vendors regained
the top tier as the meeting shifted to specification and conformance work.

Apple rose further in the rankings, now solidly in the upper tier behind the
infrastructure trio. The RRM Phase 6 work item establishment was a personal
victory for Apple's delegation, and the AI/ML mobility normative work drew
additional Apple contributions.

Keysight Technologies appeared as a significant contributor for the first time,
driven by the -Perf transition. Test equipment vendors become more active when
conformance testing work dominates, because they need to build the test systems
and their contributions define what is feasible to measure.

# Looking Ahead to RAN1#124

RAN4#117 was the meeting where RAN4 joined the 6G conversation. After five
meetings of Rel-19 delivery, Dallas marked the beginning of RAN4's own Rel-20
program. The FS_6G_Radio study item would grow through subsequent meetings. The
new Rel-20 work items (AI/ML mobility, RRM Phase 6, IoT NTN Phase 2) would
expand.

The next meeting in the series is RAN1#124 in Gothenburg, where RAN1 would
continue its 6G work at a new level of intensity: FS_6G_Radio would explode to
become the majority of the meeting. The RAN1-RAN4 dialogue was now two-way:
RAN1 designed the features, RAN4 provided the RF feasibility constraints, and
the liaison statements flowing between the groups carried the technical
positions that would shape 6G.
